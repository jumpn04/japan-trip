持ち物リスト
Packing for July Heat
Must-Have
◆Compact folding umbrella — not optional. Sudden downpours are common. Japan sells great ones for ¥1,000 at 7-Eleven.
◆Portable hand fan — ubiquitous in Japan; pick one up Day 1. Temples and shrines have zero A/C.
◆Sunscreen SPF 50+ — UV is extreme all month. Reapply every 2 hours when outdoors.
◆Electrolyte packets — heat exhaustion is a real risk with Tokyo humidity. Mix into water bottles daily.
◆Cooling towels — instant-cool towels from CVS or Amazon. Lifesaver at outdoor sites.
Clothing
◆Light, breathable fabrics — cotton, linen, or moisture-wicking. Dark colors show sweat less.
◆One warm layer per person — for the Mt. Fuji 5th station (60°F/15°C). A light zip-up is enough.
◆Slip-on shoes — temples and traditional sites require removing shoes constantly. Avoid lace-ups.
◆Wide-brim hat or cap — especially for Mt. Fuji, Nara deer park, and Fushimi Inari.
◆For teamLab Planets — shorts or pants you can roll above the knee (water room). Light and dry-quick.
